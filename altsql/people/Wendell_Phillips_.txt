Wendell Phillips ~1~1~7~Phillips visited Concord on many occasions and he was well acquainted with the town\'s abolitionists, including Mary Merrick Brooks, Bronson Alcott and the Hoar and Thoreau families. The minutes of the Concord Lyceum report that Phillips appeared there six times between 1838 and 1860. In addition, he also attended teas and other social functions organized by the Concord Female Anti-Slavery Society.
